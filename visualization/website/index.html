Airway - Project

The airway project

We are airway-team, a group of students studying computer science at the University of Rostock, dedicated to research of tree-like structures found in bronchi. We specifically investigated the possibility of classifying the upper left lobe structures as distinct different types.
Therefor we extracted a 3-dimensional model (example for patient Puhu) of the bronchus generated by CT-scans of patients and successfully developed an algorithm that converts the layered 2D images of the lung to a traversable tree. To achieve this, we detected the points where the bronchi split into different smaller tubes, thus creating nodes of a tree connected through the tubes themselves, which are represented by the edges. Relevant data found in this process (such as lobe type, level of a node, distance between nodes, etc.) is logged as node or edge data respectively.
The composed tree then is postprocessed, to eliminate false split detections and to prevent false lobe detection due to faulty lobe masks. To simplify analysis, we compose a tree for every single lobe in addition to the already generated graph representing the whole bronchus.

After correspondence with medical professionals we tried to detect two different types of left upper lobes: type A, which splits into three children after the root level of the lobe, and type B, which first splits into two children, and shortly afterwards splits into two nodes, again.

We managed to successfully classify the left upper lobes of 8 of 77 patients as having a type A lobe. For the remaining patients, which we classified as type B candidates, we further investigated the distance between the splits, but with inconclusive results.
